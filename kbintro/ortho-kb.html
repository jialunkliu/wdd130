Ortholinear Keyboard

If you are thinking to buy a ready-to-use ortholinear keyboard, there are not many options on the market. The main way to get an ortholinear keyboard is to build it yourselves! The only company I know that sells the complete product is the ZSA, they have three products, Planck EZ, Ergodox EZ, and the Moonlander.

There is a lot of ortholinear keyboard kit online you could find, pick one that you are interested in! The cheapest way is buying the DIY kit, in which you need to build it from soldering components to PCBs and flesh the software to the microcontroller. Most websites that sell DIY keyboard kits have a building guide for you, follow them step by step, and you should be able to build your keyboard without any issue. If you think that you can't do it, then there are also some keyboard that comes with a pre-build PCB, the only thing you need to do is find switches and keycaps and there you go! But it might cost more.

I have three ortholinear keyboards. They are a little bit more advance than just the ortholinear style, they are also split. My keyboards are: Ergodox TW, Sofle V1, and the Dactyl-ManuForm

Ergodox TW

Ergodox is a DIY keyboard project originally developed by Dox(Dominic Beauchamp). This is my first DIY keyboard, it's not cheap, it cost me around $200. This is the classic ortholinear and split keyboard. I enjoyed this keyboard a lot, but the problem is it is too big, I can't carry it around. It is hot-swappable, meaning that I can switch keys if I want.

Sofle v1

Sofle v1 is a very cool keyboard, it has a similar design to Ergodox, but it is smaller, easier to carry around, and it has two OLED screens and two knobs, this is the one that I am currently using. It also supports *low-profile switches!

Dactyl-ManuForm

The third one is Dactyl-ManuForm, this one is designed heavily on ergonomics. It is very similar to Ergodox but curved. I am currently building it. This one is harder to build. Because of the curving design, no PCB can fit this design. To connect each row and column, we need to wire them up all by hand, which required advanced knowledge of the keyboard. Not recommended for newbies. This one is also the most expensive to build. I spent more than $300 on it.
